CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED — EXERCISE ONLY PRECEDENCE: IMMEDIATE DTG: 141430ZOCT25 FM: C COY, 3 BN, 1 BDE TO: HQ 3 BN SUBJ: SITREP NUMBER 004 — OPERATION SIERRA VERDE RMKS: 1. ACTIVITIES (LAST 6 HOURS): A. 141015Z — En patrol, estimated 6 x armed personnel, observed IVO GR 459885. Patrol moved south on AXIS BRAVO. No contact. B. 141230Z — SVBIED threat warning received from LN source IVO PUEBLO VERDE. C. 141345Z — 1 Pl completed route clearance of AXIS SIERRA. Route assessed clear. 4. CURRENT STATUS: A. PERSONNEL: 87 x present. NIL WIA/KIA. B. SYSTEMS: All wpns systems operational. 2 x vehicles non-mission-capable (NMC): mechanical. Recovery requested. C. FUEL: 71%. AMMO: Adequate for 48 hrs. 5. INTELLIGENCE / CONTACTS: En activity IVO GR 459885 assessed as reconnaissance. En most likely COA: probe defensive positions before dark. Probability: HIGHLY PROBABLE (75-90%). 6. INTENTIONS: Maintain defensive posture. Increase observation on eastern approach NLT 1600Z. Next patrol: 150200ZOCT25. 7. WEATHER: Wind 045/14 kph. Vis: 6 km. Clear. BMNT: 0537Z. 8. NEXT SITREP: 142030ZOCT25. MAJ R. GOMEZ / OC C COY / 3 BN CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED — EXERCISE ONLY

Five-paragraph Brief Structure
Standard NATO format with BLUF
NATO Brief Structure
BRIEF STRUCTURE: Introduction → Aim → Recommendation → Body → Conclusions NOTE: The Recommendation appears in paragraph 3 — BEFORE the Body (supporting evidence). This follows the BLUF principle: the reader knows the recommendation before reading the argument. —————————————————————————————————— EXAMPLE BRIEF — ROUTE SECURITY ASSESSMENT INTRODUCTION This brief presents the current security assessment of AXIS SIERRA and provides a recommendation on its suitability for resupply convoys. AIM To advise the CO on whether resupply convoys should use AXIS SIERRA or AXIS BRAVO for the D+2 resupply operation. RECOMMENDATION It is recommended that all resupply convoys use AXIS BRAVO on D+2 until AXIS SIERRA has been cleared and formally reopened. BODY 4. AXIS SIERRA was last used on 121600ZOCT25. Subsequently, an IED was emplaced at GR 456789, and EOD cleared the device at 141200ZOCT25. However, secondary devices cannot be excluded. 5. AXIS BRAVO was recced on 141400ZOCT25. It is assessed as clear. FOM on AXIS BRAVO is unrestricted for wheeled vehicles. 6. En IED activity on AXIS SIERRA is HIGHLY PROBABLE (75–90%) to continue. En have used this route for IED emplacement on 3 previous occasions. CONCLUSIONS 7. AXIS SIERRA presents an unacceptable IED risk for D+2 convoys. AXIS BRAVO provides a viable and assessed-clear alternative. The recommendation at paragraph 3 stands. MAJ C. VARGAS / S2 / 3 BN DTG: 141800ZOCT25

Quick Reference — operational report writing
DO and DO NOT rules
DO — NATO report style
✓ Use DTG for all times.
✓ Use abbreviations freely in internal reports.
✓ One idea per numbered paragraph.
✓ Active voice for actions.
✓ NATO probability scale for intelligence.
✓ BLUF: state purpose in paragraph 1.
✓ Strong verbs: "assessed", "confirmed".
DO NOT — prohibited in reports
✗ Contractions (couldn't, it's).
✗ Phrasal verbs (went off, look into).
✗ Vague probability language.
✗ Excessive passive voice.
✗ Non-standard date/time formats.
